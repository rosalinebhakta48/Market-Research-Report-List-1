<p><p>The attenuators market is divided into two main types: passive and active attenuators. Passive attenuators are devices that reduce signal strength without requiring power, typically using resistive components, and are valued for their simplicity and reliability. In contrast, active attenuators utilize electronic components and power to dynamically adjust signal levels, offering greater precision and control over attenuation. Both types serve essential roles in telecommunications, audio, and other electronic applications, catering to diverse operational needs and performance requirements.</p></p>
