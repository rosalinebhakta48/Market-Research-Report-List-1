<p><p>Concrete Masonry Units (CMUs) are versatile building blocks made from concrete and aggregates. The market is divided into two primary types: load-bearing blocks and non-load bearing blocks. Load-bearing blocks are designed to support structural loads, making them essential for walls and foundations in buildings. Conversely, non-load bearing blocks are used primarily for partition walls or aesthetic purposes, providing insulation and fire resistance without supporting loads. Both types play critical roles in construction, catering to various design and structural needs.</p></p>
