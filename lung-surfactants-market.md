<p><ul><li>Intratracheal</li><li>Injectable</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The lung surfactants market includes two primary types: intratracheal and injectable surfactants. Intratracheal surfactants are administered directly into the trachea, often during procedures for respiratory conditions like neonatal respiratory distress syndrome, promoting lung function and reducing surface tension in the alveoli. Injectable surfactants are typically delivered through systemic routes, offering a less invasive alternative. Both types facilitate improved respiratory mechanics, enhance oxygenation, and are crucial in treating various pulmonary disorders, particularly in neonates and individuals with lung diseases.</p></p>
