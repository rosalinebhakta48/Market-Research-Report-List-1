<p><p>The bitterness inhibitors market is divided into two primary types: natural and artificial bitterness inhibitors. Natural bitterness inhibitors are derived from plant extracts, enzymes, or other organic sources that effectively mask or reduce bitter flavors in food and beverages. These are often preferred for their clean label appeal and additional health benefits. In contrast, artificial bitterness inhibitors are synthetic compounds designed to target and suppress bitter taste perception. They are typically used for cost-effectiveness and consistent flavor results in mass-produced products.</p></p>
