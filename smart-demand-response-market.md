<p><p>Smart Demand Response markets can be categorized into two types: voluntary and contractually mandatory. In voluntary markets, participants choose to adjust their energy consumption based on price signals or incentives, promoting flexibility without binding commitments. In contrast, contractually mandatory markets require participants to comply with predefined energy reduction terms, often outlined in agreements with utilities or grid operators. Both types aim to optimize energy use, enhance grid stability, and integrate renewable resources, yet they differ in commitment levels and operational enforcement.</p></p>
