<p><p>The railway turbocharger market is primarily categorized into two segments based on engine types: two-stroke and four-stroke engines. Two-stroke engines are known for their compact design and higher power-to-weight ratio, making them popular in specific applications like shunting locomotives. On the other hand, four-stroke engines offer better fuel efficiency and lower emissions, making them suitable for mainline and freight trains. Both types utilize turbochargers to enhance performance, improve fuel efficiency, and meet stringent environmental regulations.</p></p>
